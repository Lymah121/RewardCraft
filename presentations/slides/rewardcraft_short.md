# RewardCraft: Quick Presentation (10 minutes)

---

## Slide 1: Hook

# What if students could train AI... by breaking it?

**RewardCraft** - A tower defense game where students design AI reward functions

---

## Slide 2: The Problem

### AI education is too abstract

Students hear about AI but don't *experience* it

- Lectures explain algorithms
- Videos show results
- Students remain passive observers

**We need:** Hands-on AI design experience

---

## Slide 3: Our Approach

### Students = AI Trainers

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                     │
│    Student designs rewards          │
│              ↓                      │
│    AI learns from rewards           │
│              ↓                      │
│    Student observes behavior        │
│              ↓                      │
│    Student iterates & reflects      │
│                                     │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

---

## Slide 4: The Interface

### Three Panels Working Together

| Game Canvas | Q-Table Heatmap | Reward Designer |
|-------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Watch the AI play | See what AI "knows" | Set your values |
| Towers, enemies | State-action values | Sliders & presets |
| Real-time action | Learning visualized | Your AI's brain |

---

## Slide 5: Specification Gaming

### The "Aha!" Moment

**Student sets:**
- Kill enemy = +25 points
- Lose game = -50 points

**AI behavior:**
- Farms kills obsessively
- Ignores base defense
- High score, 0% wins!

**Student realizes:**
> "It's doing what I *said*, not what I *meant*"

---

## Slide 6: Ethics Built In

### Not a separate module - it's the game

| They Experience | They Learn |
|-----------------|------------|
| Specification gaming | Unintended consequences |
| Reward trade-offs | Value alignment |
| AI limitations | Critical AI literacy |
