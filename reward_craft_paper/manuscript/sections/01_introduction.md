# 1. Introduction

The rapid evolution of Artificial Intelligence (AI) necessitates a fundamental shift in K-12 education, moving beyond abstract concepts to practical, engaging, and accessible learning experiences. As AI increasingly permeates daily life, fostering AI literacy among young learners is paramount to equip them as informed digital citizens capable of understanding, critically evaluating, and ethically interacting with intelligent systems. This project is situated at the forefront of this critical educational transformation, focusing on the design and development of a novel game-based tool to teach Reinforcement Learning (RL) to high school students.

Traditional approaches to AI education often struggle to convey the intricate mechanics of complex algorithms in an age-appropriate manner. However, the emergence of interactive, game-based learning environments offers a powerful pedagogical avenue to demystify AI. These tools align with constructionist learning philosophies, where students actively build knowledge through hands-on creation and experimentation. A prime example of this innovative approach is the `TrainYourSnakeAI` project, which successfully enables students to directly engage with the core principles of RL by specifying reward functions and observing their direct impact on an AI agent's behavior. Its efficacy in fostering a foundational understanding of RL underscores the potential of interactive, game-based platforms.

Building upon this foundation, our research aims to contribute to the expanding field of K-12 AI education by developing a new game-based tool that combines deep, parametric interactivity with compelling, embodied visualization. Targeting high school students allows for greater complexity and sophistication in the learning experience. This tool will not only introduce students to the fundamental concepts of Reinforcement Learning but will also position them as true AI system designers, capable of creating multi-objective reward functions, debugging specification gaming behaviors, and understanding hyperparameter effects. By integrating ethical considerations directly into the gameplay, students will explore the societal implications of AI design through the consequences of their reward function choices. Through this endeavor, we seek to provide a pedagogically rigorous and engaging platform that addresses existing gaps in longitudinal studies, embedded assessment, and the transfer of learning within AI education, ultimately fostering a deeper, more intuitive form of AI literacy.
